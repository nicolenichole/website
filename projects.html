Font Changer for Dyslexia

As someone who struggles with dyslexia, I know how frustrating it can be to read dense or cluttered text online. I first discovered the OpenDyslexic font while reading on my Kindle, and it made a huge difference in how easily I could process and enjoy written content. I wanted to bring that same experience to the web, so I created this extension to let myself — and others — read more comfortably on any website.

This Chrome extension allows users to change the font of text on any webpage. It includes several font options, including OpenDyslexic, a font designed to improve readability for people with dyslexia.

Modular Tetris-inspired Shelf for Third Graders

International Baccalaureate Design & Technology Coursework

Inspired by the playful geometry of Tetris, I designed and built a modular shelving system tailored for third-grade students. The goal was to create a fun, engaging, and functional storage solution that kids could interact with. Each block-shaped module can be rearranged, allowing for customization and creativity while maintaining practicality in a classroom or bedroom setting. The design process involved user research with children and teachers, concept sketching, CAD modeling, prototyping, and material selection focused on safety and durability.
